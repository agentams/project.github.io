Welcome to Government College Solan Himachal Pradesh, Affiliated to Himachal Pradesh University Shimla-5-INDIA

Govt. College, Solan was established on 19th August 1959 and is situated in 47 Bighas of land to cater to the needs of higher education of Solan district and the surrounding areas in the state of Himachal Pradesh. It was started in a Private building near the Present D.C. Office, Solan. Initially the College had strength of about 150 students in the faculty of Arts. Faculty of Science and Commerce were added on later on. The College shifted to its Present Permanent building in 1964.This co-educational college has been progressing leaps and bound since its inception. The quality education that the college provides attracts students from different parts of the state and even adjoining states. The college has a well qualified and committed team of teachers who untiringly provide education in the streams of Humanities, Science, Commerce and Fine Arts at undergraduate level. The college also offers Post-Graduation in the subjects of English & Economics and has the honor of producing a number of college teachers and scholars. Professional courses such as BCA, BBA, PGDCA and Add-On-Course in Computer Awareness and JMC are running very successfully in the college and have helped many students compete efficiently in the present day job market. Besides being one of the most important agencies in providing formal education the college has also contributed in the field of distance and non-formal education. Since 1987, the college is running IGNOU Study Centre to provide education to those whose circumstances prevent allow them to get regular classroom education. The college laboratories are equipped with latest and up-to-date apparatuses. A well stacked library offers wide range of books in all streams. Recently, the process of computerization of library has been launched through INFLIBNE which is a UGC sponsored library automation programme. Library offers an access to E-Resources and E-Books that have been made available through N-LIST of INFLIBNET. The college has also provided internet access to every department of the college through HIMSWAN. Students of the college can also access internet facility in the library, departments and computer labs. A wide range of extra-curricular activities are done in the college such as NCC (Boys and Girls), NSS, Rangers & Rovers, Eco Club, Photography Club, cultural and sports activities help in the development of all round personality of students. Improvement in Communication Skills is another priority area of the college for which a Language Lab has been established to help the students cultivate effective communication proficiency. Women Development Cell, Ambedkar Cell & Career Guidance and Placement Cell have been established in the college to help students coming from diverse social backgrounds. College Students Central Association (CSCA), Parent Teacher Association (P.T.A.) and Old Student Association (O.S.A.) actively participate in the functioning of the college. A number of clubs formed in the college provide opportunity to the students to explore their talents.




Procedure of Admission

Choice-based credit system (CBCS) has been introduced in HP colleges under Rashtirya Uchhtar Shiksha Abhiyan (RUSA). Choice Based Credit System (CBCS) is an instructional package developed to suit the needs of students to keep pace with the developments in higher education worldwide taking him/her out of isolated compartmentalized learning environment which clearly indicates for each level of certification learning effort required on the part of the student in terms of credits to be earned as well as participation in instructional hours to be spent through either regular mode or through distance education mode. Admissions seekers will have to submit filled forms by due date as announced by the university time to time and a merit list would be displayed. Under Choice-based credit system, a student will have to opt for a major subject and the college will then display the merit list after going through the percentage of the number of students opting for that particular course. Students will also be helped by carrier and guidance cell and admission committees to choose minor, compulsory, additional and courses of hobby and general interest. The system also has a significant element of teacher evaluation by students to foster a responsibility of the teacher towards what is being taught, and provisions like spoken tutorials will also be available for students under the system.

Eligibility for Admission

  1. A pass in the Senior Secondary (+2) Examination (Academic Stream), conducted by the H.P. Board of School Education or an examination accepted as equivalent thereto by the various bodies of the H.P.University. Provided that the candidates who have passed the qualifying examination with science stream shall be given preference in admission to B.Sc. programme. Provided that the candidates who have passed the qualifying examination with commerce stream shall be given preference in admission to B.Com. Programme.

  2. An applicant must not be more than 23 years (25 years for girls) of age as on 01-07-2013 and must not have been born earlier to 01.07.1990. However in age of overage candidates, the Vice-Chancellor may allow age relaxation up to six months. In case of Scheduled Caste / Scheduled Tribe, student’s relaxation up to three years is allowed as per rules.

  3. An applicant failing in all subjects or having a compartment in 10+2 will not be admitted.

1. COMPULSORY COURSES: A student enrolled in an undergraduate programme will have to pass some compulsory courses. These compulsory courses are of four kinds:

(a) Languages-

  • Compulsory English
  • Compulsory Hindi

(b) Social Science/ Commerce -

  • Compulsory Social Science/ commerce/ management course
  • Compulsory Geography of Himachal Pradesh 
  • Compulsory Indian constitution
  • Compulsory Himachal Past, Present and Future.

(c) Skill Based Courses-

  • Functional English
  • Functional Hindi

A minimum of three and a maximum of six compulsory courses are to be passed by all students enrolled for a UG degree. However,
All compulsory courses will be three credit hours each. Thus, a maximum of 3 x 6 = 18 credit hours will be accumulated by a student by doing compulsory courses.
A minimum of three compulsory courses (3 x 3 = 9 credits) will have to be done by each UG student to qualify for the UG degree.

2. MAJOR SUBJECT (CORE) COURSES

Core Courses can be of two kinds:

Hard Core Courses, which have to be done without exception. There will be no choice available. Soft Core Courses, in which some degree of choice is available in the sense that he / she has to select a certain number of courses out of the given number (larger than the required ) of courses. The student will have to choose a certain number of core courses out of the total available.

ln each subject there will be a minimum of fourteen (14) core courses (hard and/ or soft). Each core course will be of four credit hours. Thus, a student majoring in the concerned subject will accumulate 14 x 4 = 56 credit hours by passing core courses.

A student will be required to pass in a minimum of 14 core courses in the subject of his / her major to qualify for a UG degree in a subject (Major).

3. ELECTIVE COURSES:

All students enrolled for a UG degree will be required to pass a minimum of between 10 and 13 elective courses.

  • However, a student may take more elective courses for the purpose of ‘Emphasis’ or ‘Double Major.
  • Elective courses are to be from a student’s major / minor subject(s) as detailed above in the subject combinations allowed.
  • Each UG student will have to pass at least 10 to 13 elective courses from his/her in minor (allied) subjects being taught in the college.
  • Provided that each student will have to take a minimum of 5 elective courses in either of his /her minor subjects.
  • Each elective course will be 4 credit hours. Thus, a student will be able to accumulate at least 10 x 4 = 40 credits by passing elective courses.

4. GENERAL INTEREST AND HOBBY ( GI & COURSES)

  • College will offer some general interest (GI) and / or hobby (H) courses cutting across subjects.
  • The college administration will decide on the basis of its material and human resources as to what such courses can be offered.
  • Each course will be one credit hour. Thus, a student will be able to accumulate at least 3 x 1 = 3 credits by passing these courses.
